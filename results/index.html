Structural Implications

PM identity is structurally determined

M's direction affects coalition composition but not PM identity. Whether Løkke leans toward S, V, or stays independent, Frederiksen's PM probability remains effectively unchanged.

mPmPref = S → PM(S) = 97.7%  |  mPmPref = V → PM(S) = 98.0%  |  mPmPref = M → PM(S) = 97.7%

The PM-demand mechanism produces gridlock, not power transfer

When M demands the PM post, the blocked government formations do not flow to blue alternatives. Instead, gridlock rises from 0.4% to 14.8%, while blue government probability barely moves (2.7% → 2.2%).

mDemandPM = true → gridlock = 14.8%, blue = 2.2%

Coalition preference reshapes government type without changing leadership

Sweeping Frederiksen's red preference from 0.2 to 0.8 shifts the modal coalition from S+SF+RV+M (broad, at 0.2) to S+SF (narrow red, at 0.8), but PM(S) stays at approximately 97% throughout.

redPref = 0.2 → PM(S) = 97.2%, top: S+SF+RV+M  |  redPref = 0.8 → PM(S) = 97.0%, top: S+SF

Legislative fragility varies sharply across coalition types

At baseline, S+SF has an average passage probability of 0.75, while S+SF+RV+M achieves 0.88. The broader coalition is 13 percentage points more legislatively secure, reflecting the arithmetic advantage of including M's mandates.

S+SF pPassage = 0.754  |  S+SF+RV = 0.815  |  S+SF+M = 0.820  |  S+SF+RV+M = 0.877

The market price emerges from the interaction space

Polymarket's ~80% for Frederiksen matches the mDemandPM = true scenario (83.0%). The three-way interaction (M demands PM + blue formateur + blue-strong mandates) produces 54.0%, the lower bound. The baseline of 96.9% represents the modal structural expectation absent political disruption.

Baseline: 96.9%  |  mDemandPM: 83.0%  |  Full interaction: 54.0%
How the Model Works — Technical Overview
Stage 1
Draw an Election Night

Correlated mandate draws: bloc-level shock + within-bloc substitution + per-party noise. Example: "Polls say S=38 → this iteration drew S=41, SF=21 (substitution: when S goes up, SF goes down within the red bloc)."

Technical detail

Correlation structure. Three layers of noise are drawn independently each iteration and summed:

  1. Bloc-level shock (σbloc = 4.0 mandates): one draw per bloc (red, blue), applied equally to every party in that bloc. Captures correlated polling error.
  2. Within-bloc substitution pairs (σsub = 1.5): paired zero-sum noise between allied parties. Pairs: S ↔ SF, V ↔ LA, DF ↔ DD. When one gains, the partner loses the same amount.
  3. Per-party noise (σparty = 1.5): independent Gaussian noise on each party. Captures idiosyncratic polling error.

Swing party weights. Parties not firmly in either bloc are allocated probabilistically to red or blue each iteration:

  • M (Moderaterne): red = 0.40, blue = 0.40, independent = 0.20
  • RV (Radikale Venstre): red = 0.70, blue = 0.15, independent = 0.15
  • KF (Konservative): red = 0.15, blue = 0.70, independent = 0.15

Threshold parties. Parties near the 2% electoral threshold are modeled with a Bernoulli draw for clearing it:

  • ALT (Alternativet): baseVoteShare = 2.1%, pClear = 0.60
  • BP (Borgelig Parti / Nye Borgerlige remnant): baseVoteShare = 2.8%, pClear = 0.75

North Atlantic (NA) seat alignment. The 4 NA seats (2 Greenland, 2 Faroe) are drawn independently each iteration:

  • GL1: pRed = 0.60, pFlex = 0.30, pBlue = 0.10
  • GL2: pRed = 0.25, pFlex = 0.45, pBlue = 0.30
  • FO1: pRed = 0.55, pFlex = 0.20, pBlue = 0.25
  • FO2: pRed = 0.10, pFlex = 0.15, pBlue = 0.75

Normalization. After applying all noise, mandates are rounded and rescaled so the total always equals exactly 175 Danish seats + 4 NA seats = 179. Negative mandates are floored at 0.

Stage 2
Generate Policy Platforms

5 policy dimensions × 2–3 options = 162 platform combinations. Grid: taxation (formueskat / substitute / none) × forståelsespapir (yes / no) × green investment (high / med / low) × immigration (restrictive / moderate / status quo) × fiscal stance (expansive / moderate / tight).

Technical detail

The full 5-dimensional grid:

  1. Taxation (3 levels): formueskat (wealth tax — red signal), substitute (alternative revenue measure — midter signal), none (no new taxes — blue signal)
  2. Forståelsespapir (2 levels): yes (formal coalition agreement), no (informal support arrangement)
  3. Green investment (3 levels): high, medium, low
  4. Immigration (3 levels): restrictive, moderate, status_quo
  5. Fiscal stance (3 levels): expansive, moderate, tight

Total: 3 × 2 × 3 × 3 × 3 = 162 combinations.

Coherence constraints prune impossible or incoherent combinations (from sim3-packages.js). For example, formueskat + tight fiscal is contradictory; none + expansive + status_quo immigration is incoherent for a red platform. After pruning, roughly 80–100 platforms survive per coalition type.

Platform signal effects on party behavior:

  • forståelsespapir = yes → M (Moderaterne) incurs a penalty (they prefer informal arrangements)
  • immigration = restrictive → EL and RV incur a penalty (ideologically opposed)
  • taxation = none + fiscal = tight → SF and EL incur a “right-tilt” penalty (perceived as abandoning core commitments)
  • green = high → small bonus for SF, EL; small penalty for DF, DD

TOP_K = 8 heuristic pre-filter. For each candidate government, only the 8 highest-scoring platforms (by ideology fit) proceed to the full DP budget passage computation. This reduces computation from 162 × 11 DP calls to 8 × 11 per iteration without meaningfully affecting results, since low-fit platforms almost never produce the top score.

Stage 3
Compute Budget Passage Probability

A dynamic programming algorithm: for each non-government party, branch into FOR / AGAINST / ABSTAIN with drawn probabilities. Accumulate across all parties. Passage requires FOR > AGAINST and FOR ≥ 70. Example: "With S+SF in government (61 mandates voting FOR), the DP checks whether enough other parties also vote FOR to beat the opposition."

Technical detail

State space. The DP table is dp[forVotes][againstVotes], where both dimensions run from 0 to total mandates (179). Each cell holds the probability of reaching that exact (FOR, AGAINST) count after processing all non-government parties.

Per-party branching. For each non-government party with m mandates, the DP processes three branches weighted by (pFor, pAgainst, pAbstain):

  • FOR branch: shifts state by (+m, 0), weighted by pFor
  • AGAINST branch: shifts state by (0, +m), weighted by pAgainst
  • ABSTAIN branch: shifts state by (0, 0), weighted by pAbstain

Passage condition: FOR > AGAINST and FOR ≥ minForVotes (default 70). The final P(passage) is the sum of all dp[f][a] where f > a and f ≥ 70.

EL-M sequential conditioning. Enhedslisten (EL) and Moderaterne (M) have correlated budget behavior. The DP is run three times with different M probabilities, conditional on EL’s stance:

  • Branch 1: EL = FOR → M is penalized (lower pFor for M, higher pAgainst)
  • Branch 2: EL = ABSTAIN → M at baseline probabilities
  • Branch 3: EL = AGAINST → M is boosted (higher pFor for M, signaling centrist budget)

Final P(passage) = weighted sum of the three branches, weighted by EL’s (pFor, pAbstain, pAgainst).

The mDemandPM mechanism. When Løkke demands the PM post and is in a government where he is not PM, M’s mandates are treated as rebelling: routed through the DP with pFor = 0, pAgainst = 0.98, pAbstain = 0.02. This tanks the passage probability for any government containing M where M is not the formateur.

Key P(FOR) ranges by party (approximate, vary by platform):

  • SF: 0.60–0.85 (high for red platforms, lower if not in government but ideologically close)
  • EL: 0.30–0.65 (depends heavily on platform — formueskat boosts, restrictive immigration tanks)
  • M: 0.20–0.55 (depends on EL conditioning and platform signals)
  • RV: 0.45–0.75 (generally supportive of S-led budgets unless immigration is restrictive)
  • DF: 0.10–0.40 (rarely supports red budgets unless immigration is restrictive)
  • KF: 0.15–0.35 (rarely supports red budgets)

Abstain/against split. When a party does not vote FOR, the remaining probability is split between AGAINST and ABSTAIN. The split ratio varies by party: EL has a higher abstain share (protest via abstention rather than active opposition), while blue parties lean more toward AGAINST.

Stage 4
Score and Rank Coalitions

Score = P(passage) × preference weight × ideology fit × size bonus × MWCC bonus × historical precedent. The highest-scoring viable package wins. "The government with the best combination of legislative viability, ideological coherence, and Frederiksen's preference wins."

Technical detail

Multiplicative scoring formula:

score = P(passage) × preferenceWeight × ideologyFit × sizeBonus × mwccBonus × historicalPrecedent

Each component:

  • P(passage): the DP-computed budget passage probability from Stage 3.
  • Preference weight: reflects Frederiksen’s coalition preference. For S-led governments:
    • Pure red (S+SF, S+SF+RV): 1 + redPreference × 0.3
    • Midter/broad (S+SF+RV+M, S+SF+M): 1 + (1 − redPreference) × 0.3
    • Lognormal noise with σ = 0.1 is applied to introduce stochastic variation in preferences across iterations
  • Ideology fit: exp(−distPenalty × meanAbsoluteLRDistance), with a floor of 0.3. The LR distance is the average absolute left-right distance between the formateur and each coalition partner, weighted by mandates.
  • Size bonus: exp(−sizePenalty × (nMembers − 1)), with a floor of 0.5. Rewards parsimony — fewer parties in government means fewer veto players.
  • MWCC bonus:
    • 1.15 — full minimum winning connected coalition (MWCC): connected on LR spectrum + minimal winning
    • 1.08 — connected and small (connected on LR spectrum but not strictly minimal winning)
    • 1.05 — connected only
    • 1.00 — none of the above
  • Historical precedent: 1 + score × 0.02 per precedent point. Coalitions resembling past Danish governments (e.g., S+RV has historical precedent from several governments) get a small multiplicative boost.

Formateur protocol. By default, S (Socialdemokratiet) evaluates first as formateur, reflecting the convention that the largest party gets the first attempt. With probability pBlueFormateur, the blue bloc gets the formateur mandate instead, and V or LA evaluates first. The formateur evaluates all 11 candidate governments and picks the highest-scoring one.

Stage 5
Repeat 1,000+ Times and Count

Aggregation: PM probabilities, government type frequencies, top coalition rankings. "After 1,000–5,000 simulated election nights, we count: how often does each party leader become PM? What is the most common government type?"

Technical detail

Outer loop. N iterations per configuration (750–5,000). Each iteration is independent.

Per-iteration pipeline:

  1. Draw mandates (Stage 1 correlation structure)
  2. Draw budget basis probabilities (per-party pFor/pAgainst/pAbstain, with noise)
  3. Draw NA seat alignment (4 independent Bernoulli draws)
  4. Call selectGovernment(): evaluate all 11 candidate governments × top-K platforms, compute DP passage for each, score, pick winner
  5. Record outcome: PM identity, government type, winning coalition, passage probability, platform

Aggregation. After all N iterations:

  • PM frequencies: count of iterations where each party leader becomes PM, as percentages
  • Government type frequencies: red, blue, cross-bloc, gridlock (none), S-alone, midter
  • Top coalition rankings: ordered by frequency, with average P(passage) for each
  • Best platform: the most common winning platform for each coalition type

Confidence check. Before accepting a government, a pre-filter checks that the opposition mandates voting FOR a mistillidsvotum (vote of no confidence) would be < 90. This is a structural sanity check, not a binding constraint — it filters out arithmetically impossible governments.

Sweep methodology. The sensitivity analysis uses one-at-a-time (OAT) parameter variation plus targeted interaction crosses:

  • Pass 1: 191 configurations at N = 750 for broad coverage of the parameter space
  • Pass 2: 61 key configurations re-run at N = 5,000 for precision on important scenarios
  • Transitions: 54 fine-grained runs at N = 5,000 exploring phase boundaries (e.g., what polling error flips PM identity)
  • Where labels overlap across passes, the higher-N result is used
  • Interaction crosses: pairwise combinations of mDemandPM × mandate archetype × pBlueFormateur, plus redPref × flex, to map the interaction surface
Stage 6
Key Assumptions and Limitations

The model makes structural simplifications that bound the interpretation of results. Understanding these is essential for using the outputs responsibly.

Technical detail

Parties as probability distributions, not strategic actors. Each party’s budget voting behavior is drawn from a fixed probability distribution. Parties do not anticipate others’ moves, negotiate sequentially, or update beliefs. This means the model cannot capture strategic dynamics like “EL threatens to vote against unless formueskat is included.”

Parallel evaluation, not sequential bargaining. The formateur evaluates all 11 candidate governments simultaneously and picks the best score. In reality, government formation is a sequential process with failed attempts, new mandates, and evolving negotiation positions. The model compresses this into a single optimization step.

The “substitute” taxation level is a modeling construct. The three taxation levels (formueskat / substitute / none) are an abstraction. “Substitute” represents an alternative revenue measure that is less ideologically charged than a wealth tax but still generates revenue. It does not correspond to a specific real-world policy proposal.

Fixed party positions within each iteration. Within a single iteration, party positions and preferences are fixed. There is no intra-iteration negotiation or position adjustment. Variation comes from across-iteration noise, not within-iteration dynamics.

No nyvalg (snap re-election) threat modeling. The model does not include the possibility of a government calling a snap election as a bargaining tool. In practice, the threat of nyvalg can discipline coalition partners.

The 11 enumerated government packages. The model evaluates a fixed set of 11 candidate governments:

  • Red-bloc: S+SF, S+SF+RV, S+SF+RV+M, S+SF+M
  • Cross-bloc: S+V, S+V+M
  • Blue-bloc: V-led blue, LA-led blue
  • Minority: S-alone
  • Broad: S+RV (historical)
  • Other combinations as defined in sim3-packages.js

Notable exclusions: no M-led governments (M is too small to lead), no governments including EL (they have never been in government and have stated they will not join one), no governments including DF (similar structural constraints).

The calibration problem. P(FOR) ranges for each party are informed by expert judgment and historical voting patterns, but they are not empirically validated against a large sample of actual budget votes for all parties. The EL-M conditioning structure, for instance, is based on qualitative observation of their political interdependence rather than a formal statistical estimate. Results should be interpreted as “given these assumed probability ranges, what follows structurally” rather than “this is the true probability.”
