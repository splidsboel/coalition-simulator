# The 2026 Danish election: board state and formation landscape

This document synthesizes everything specific to the March 24, 2026 election: polling, party positions, coalition constraints, expert assessments, and scenario analysis. It is a research brief for someone who needs to understand the full strategic landscape heading into the formation process.

---

## 1. Election context

**Date:** Tuesday 24 March 2026. Snap election (udskrivelse), called by PM Frederiksen from the Folketinget podium on 26 February 2026. The constitutional deadline was approximately 30 October 2026. The SVM government (S+V+M) did not dissolve or collapse -- it sat the entire election period and remains formally in office until a new government is formed. The call was strategically timed after passage of the foedevarecheck and launch of S's formueskat proposal.

**Seat structure:** 179 total (175 Danish + 2 Greenland + 2 Faroe Islands). Majority threshold: 90 mandater. Effective threshold (spaerregraense): 2% of national vote.

**Parties on the ballot:** 12 opstillingsberettigede parties.

## 3. Threshold parties

**Alternativet** stands at 2.4% -- just 0.4 percentage points above the 2% threshold, well within polling error. If ALT falls below 2%, approximately 4 mandater vanish from red bloc (86 to 82). Multiple analysts call this potentially election-deciding.

**Borgernes Parti** stands at 2.7% -- somewhat safer but still within error bounds. Trend rising, with individual polls showing BP at 3.3%. If BP falls below 2%, approximately 5 mandater vanish from blue bloc (80 to 75).

## 4. North Atlantic seats

The four North Atlantic seats may determine the election outcome. The traditional assumption of approximately 3 red + 1 blue no longer holds.

### Greenland (2 seats)

Since 2001, Greenland has sent two red-aligned mandates to the Folketing -- a 25-year streak now under threat. Both incumbent Greenlandic MFs (Aaja Chemnitz from IA and Aki-Matilda Hoegh-Dam from Naleraq) are not re-standing.

Trump's renewed interest in Greenland has transformed the campaign. All Greenlandic parties are leveraging geopolitical attention to demand greater autonomy, making their support highly transactional.

**Inuit Ataqatigiit (IA)** has fielded Naaja H. Nathanielsen and returning heavyweight Kuupik Kleist. IA demands a vetoret for Greenland in foreign policy and reform of the Rigsfaellesskab. IA leans red historically but has not pre-committed to a PM preference.

**Siumut** has made the most dramatic shift. Under party chair Aleqa Hammond, Siumut is deliberately uncommitted on PM, stating they will cooperate with whoever delivers the most for Greenland. Siumut is in its deepest crisis ever: it collapsed to 4 seats (from 10) at the 2025 Inatsisartut election, pulled out of the Greenlandic coalition government in mid-March 2026, and lost two of its biggest figures. The transactional posture reflects existential weakness.

**Demokraatit** (Liberal Alliance's sister party, leads current Greenland government) fields Anna Wangenheim, a strong personal vote-getter. Despite being "blue," Wangenheim has confirmed openness to pointing at Frederiksen. Could deliver the first blue Greenlandic mandate in over 15 years.

**Naleraq** under Pele Broberg is fully transactional and explicitly bloc-neutral.

### Faroe Islands (2 seats)

**Javnadarflokkurin** (Social Democrats): Sjurdur Skaale is running again despite earlier farewell signals. Expected to point to Frederiksen.

**Sambandsflokkurin** (Union Party): Anna Falkenberg would point to the Venstre PM candidate.

**Wildcard:** If Folkaflokkurin (People's Party), polling strongly for the Lagtingsvalg, captures a Folketing seat instead of Javnadarflokkurin, the Faroes could deliver 2 blue mandates -- unprecedented in recent history.

### The 2022 precedent

The 2022 election demonstrated the North Atlantic mandates' decisive potential. The NA seats gave red bloc exactly 90 mandater (87 Danish + 3 NA). Loekke raged on election night: "Because of the vote in Greenland, there is a red majority in Denmark." He reportedly called Aaja Chemnitz (IA) that night trying to flip her -- she refused. Blue bloc + M received 62,501 more votes from Denmark proper, yet lost on mandates due to the North Atlantic configuration.

### Combined North Atlantic assessment

The old 3-red/1-blue baseline is unreliable for 2026. The most likely outcome is 2 red, 1 uncertain, 1 blue -- but scenarios range from 4 red to 1 red/3 blue. If red bloc gets 86 Danish seats, they need 4 North Atlantic seats to reach 90 (now very unlikely), 3 to reach 89, 2 to reach 88. The fewer reliable red North Atlantic seats, the stronger the Moderaterne kingmaker role becomes. A new dynamic is the post-Trump/Greenland crisis assertiveness -- NA mandates will push harder than ever on rigsfaellesskab reform, defense agreement renegotiation, and autonomy demands, regardless of which bloc they support.

## 5. Party-by-party position summaries

### 5.1 Socialdemokratiet (Frederiksen) -- 38 mandater

Frederiksen's strategy is dual-track: she has said both a red government and a midterregering are possible, closing no doors. In practice, her campaign has leaned heavily red. Key proposals -- formueskat (wealth tax) on net fortunes above a threshold, smaller class sizes of 14 students, expanded Arne-pension -- are policies only achievable with left-bloc partners. A DR survey of 119 S municipal councillors found overwhelming preference for a red government.

Yet Frederiksen has hedged the wealth tax, telling Jyllands-Posten it is "not a goal in itself" and explicitly stating it is not an ultimative demand. She is willing to replace it with alternative financing. This pre-concession effectively removes the single biggest policy obstacle to S+M cooperation.

**Key policy signals:** Formueskat launched but explicitly not ultimative -- willing to replace it. Store Bededag reinstatement expected in negotiations. Klasseloft (14 pupils) is the flagship velvaerd proposal. Expanded Arne-pension. On defense: broad consensus on 3.5% GDP spending. On immigration: continuation of S's stram linje.

S will only lead a government -- never serve as stoetteparti or junior partner. Frederiksen has explicitly refused to point to Loekke as PM, even in a hypothetical where she gets all her policy demands in return. She would actively oppose any blue-led government. There is no scenario in which S passively tolerates a blue PM.

### 5.2 SF (Olsen Dyhr) -- 23 mandater

SF's position is the clearest and most consequential. Olsen Dyhr has flatly rejected a repeat of the stoetteparti arrangement: government or nothing. She has publicly demanded the Finance Ministry -- or at minimum a senior economic portfolio. SF categorically rejects governing with Venstre or Konservative but is open to Moderaterne and Radikale in a red-framework government.

**Policy conditions:**
- Restore Store Bededag (first act of new government)
- Welfare restoration, green transition
- 8 billion kr groundwater fund, total pesticide ban on vulnerable areas
- Class size caps requiring 5,000+ additional teachers
- Formueskat and higher stock income taxation
- Absolute red line: "never again accept borgerlig oekonomisk politik"

**PM preference is conditional.** SF points to Frederiksen if she commits to a red government. SF categorically rejects pointing to Loekke.

**The toleration question.** During the 2025 B16 mistillidsafstemning, SF abstained rather than voting for or against -- suggesting a strategic middle ground. Under negative parliamentarism, SF could refuse to participate in or support a midterregering while not actively blocking its formation. The signals suggest SF would be loud opposition but passive tolerance of an S+M government.

**SF's strength is historically exceptional.** The party won the 2024 European Parliament election in Denmark (17.4%), had strong 2025 municipal results, and polls at approximately 13% (roughly 23 mandater) -- compared to 7.7% in 2019 and 4.2% in 2015. The 2011 trauma -- when SF entered the Thorning government and was devastated -- is the single strongest anchor in SF's institutional memory. Olsen Dyhr became leader precisely because of that catastrophe and has built her entire political identity around not repeating it.

### 5.3 Enhedslisten (Dragsted) -- 12 mandater

In a historic break announced in December 2025, EL declared it would not point to Frederiksen as PM or forhandlingsleder unless she commits unambiguously to a red government. Dragsted has endorsed Pia Olsen Dyhr as an alternative kongelig undersoeger.

**Stoetteparti conditions.** EL demands a forstaelsespapir with inequality reduction as an ultimativt krav. The comprehensive demand portfolio spans three tiers:
- **Tier 1 red lines:** The forstaelsespapir itself, addressing inequality, and a 1% wealth tax on net fortunes above 35M DKK (estimated ~10 billion kr/year)
- **Tier 2 core demands:** Halved food VAT, eliminated fruit/vegetable VAT, binding biodiversity law, rent control, improved Arne-pension, restoration of Store Bededag, free dental care/prescription medicine
- **Tier 3 aspirational:** Higher personal tax deduction for low incomes, 15% housing gains tax, Danmarksbillet, 90% CO2 reduction by 2035

Compared to 2019, the demands are more quantified, more focused on cost-of-living, and notably silent on immigration. Dragsted has strategically avoided making immigration a red line despite grassroots pressure. The wealth tax at precisely 1%/35M DKK is likely a negotiating anchor given Dragsted's acknowledgment that inequality "kan goeres paa mange maader."

**The toleration question.** EL's threat to topple the government applies specifically to a red government that fails on inequality -- not to a center government where their mandates are not part of the basis. The most likely modeling assumption: EL would refuse to support an S+M midterregering but would not actively vote it down. Historically, EL tolerated the Nyrup governments (1994-2001) despite disagreement.

**EL learned from the Thorning experience.** After being sidelined during 2011-2015, the party adopted new parliamentary principles giving explicit authority to topple future red governments that cross red lines. Pernille Skipper said in 2019: "If we had known then what we know now, we should have toppled her." The 2019 forstaelsespapir innovation was a direct response.

### 5.4 Radikale Venstre (Lidegaard) -- 9 mandater

RV explicitly seeks government seats. Lidegaard envisions a center-left government: S+SF+RV. This is RV's first choice. RV opposes the formueskat but considers it "not a stopklods" for cooperation. On green policy, RV has a joint demand with SF, EL, and ALT: a total pesticide ban on vulnerable groundwater-forming areas as a precondition for supporting a new government.

**Key demands:** Denmark's greenest government ever, total pesticide ban on vulnerable groundwater, massive education investment, writing the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child into Danish law, removing registreringsafgift on elbiler.

**Blue-side flexibility.** Lidegaard does not exclude that RV mandates can support a blue PM, stating that if a blue majority emerges without RV, the party would explore replacing DF in such a constellation -- arguing this "would be best for Denmark." However, Troels Lund Poulsen has explicitly ruled out governing with RV, calling them untrustworthy after the 2022 walkout. This mistrust is shared by S and M. The blue door is closed from the other side.

**RV's only hard exclusion is DF.** All other parties are theoretically available cooperation partners.

**Kingmaker potential is limited.** Rostrup identified both RV and M as potential kingmakers but doubted RV could truly play the role because it would struggle to support a pure blue government. Unlike M, which can genuinely swing both ways, RV's blue optionality is constrained by V's distrust and by policy distance on green/education issues. RV + M alignment on anti-formueskat and anti-flank-dependency creates a natural midterregering nucleus.

### 5.5 Moderaterne (Loekke) -- 9 mandater

Neither bloc reaches 90 mandater without M, making Loekke the structural kingmaker. A YouGov model gives red bloc + M a combined majority with >99% probability, while adding M to blue bloc succeeds in only roughly 10%.

**Government demand is firm.** M demands ministerial seats -- being a stoetteparti is incompatible with their identity as a governing centrist project.

**Red lines:**
- Will NOT support a government dependent on Enhedslisten and Alternativet
- Will NOT support a government with DF in the parliamentary basis
- Formueskat rejected -- Loekke called it "et populistisk forslag fra nederste hylde"

**The PM question is deliberately ambiguous.** Loekke has mused that the non-S parties could collectively provide the PM. He then walked it back. On March 21, he told DR he could "see more of my own politics" in what Troels Lund Poulsen is saying -- a possible late-campaign signal toward the blue side. Most analysts read the strategic ambiguity as genuine positioning rather than confused signaling: Loekke's party was created precisely for this kingmaker position.

**M would actively oppose a "sortblaadt" (dark blue) government reliant on DF, and would actively oppose a "hoejroedt" (deep red) government reliant on EL/ALT.**

**Arithmetical implications of Loekke's constraints:**

| Constellation | Danish mandater (approx.) | Loekke-compatible? | Notes |
|--------------|--------------------------|-------------------|-------|
| S+SF+EL+RV+ALT (pure red) | 86 | N/A -- M not needed if this reaches 90 with NA seats | M is bypassed entirely |
| S+SF (+ EL/ALT support) + M | 70 + support | NO | M won't enable far-left dependency |
| S+M+V (SVM reconstituted) | 64 | YES in principle | Arithmetically dead -- far from 90 |
| S+M+SF+RV | 79 | YES -- M's sweet spot | Needs EL tolerance + NA; not EL dependency |
| S+M+V+KF | 76 | YES in principle | But SF excluded by V/KF presence |
| V+LA+KF+M (blue with M) | 57 | Partially | Far short; adding DD (70), still need DF (84) but M vetoes DF |
| V+LA+KF+DD+DF (pure blue) | 80 | NO | M vetoes DF |

**Others' exclusions of Loekke:** DF sees "no reason for Lars Loekke to have any influence on Denmark whatsoever" -- pure personal veto. DD has issued a "resounding no" to partnership. Both DF and DD block Loekke from any blue-led government.

**Loekke's realistic aspiration** is continued ministerial influence (likely udenrigsminister again) plus policy vetoes in a midterregering. A formal PM candidacy is acknowledged as "extremely unrealistic" even by Loekke himself. No other party endorses him for PM. Frederiksen has explicitly refused to point to Loekke as PM even in a hypothetical where she gets all her policy demands in return.

### 5.6 Venstre (Troels Lund Poulsen) -- 17 mandater

TLP declared his PM candidacy on February 26. His first priority is a pure blue government. His second priority is a "blaa midterregering" -- a government of V+LA+KF+M without S -- which he floated on March 21-22 via Facebook, calling it "naturligt" for KF, LA, and M to participate. He has not fully excluded governing with Frederiksen again, having been "vaccinated" against issuing an ultimative rejection after learning from Jakob Ellemann-Jensen's 2022 mistake.

He has ruled out governing with RV (trust deficit from the 2022 walkout), SF, EL, ALT, and Borgernes Parti.

**Intra-blue tension with LA.** V and LA are competing intensely for blue-bloc leadership. If LA outpolls V on election night, the PM candidacy question is genuinely open. Vanopslagh has insisted the largest blue party should become kongelig undersoeger. V polls at approximately 10% (17 mandater), while LA polls at 11-12% (19 mandater). Analysts note V at 8.6% would break the 1987 record of 10.5% -- the worst result in the party's history.

**Policy signals:** Selskabsskat cut (22% to 20%), 25 billion DKK velvaerd increase, ulandsbistand cut (0.7% to 0.5%), statsborgerretskonvention withdrawal, seniorpension reform.

**Campaign performance.** TLP had a slow start: five and a half hours into the campaign before formally declaring as PM candidate. His early performances were described as "kluntet" (clumsy). Store Bededag disagreement with the rest of blue bloc created early friction.

### 5.7 Konservative (Mona Juul) -- 12 mandater

Mona Juul (leader since March 2024, following Soeren Pape Poulsen's death on March 2, 2024) committed KF to entering government "for alt i verden." First choice is blue under TLP, but she has not ruled out a "lyseroede" (light-red/pink) center government with Frederiksen.

KF's green positioning -- supporting sproejtforbud on vulnerable groundwater areas -- creates partial alignment with red-bloc environmental demands, making KF the most plausible blue party to cross into a midterregering including S+SF. KF proposes 34.35 billion kr in tax/duty relief and cutting corporate tax from 22% to 17%. Strong defense positioning.

KF's breaking from the blaa samling for the FL2026 bilateral deal with SVM was described as "opsigtsvaekkende" (remarkable) by DR -- a signal that Mona Juul's KF may be willing to cooperate pragmatically across bloc lines when it suits the party's interests.

All three traditional blue parties (V, KF, LA) would go into opposition if a red government forms but are extremely unlikely to initiate a mistillidsvotum -- they would be constructive opposition.

### 5.8 Liberal Alliance (Vanopslagh) -- 19 mandater

LA declared Vanopslagh a PM candidate conditional on being the largest blue party. Polling at 11-12%, LA sometimes leads blue bloc, making this demand credible. Vanopslagh endorsed the blaa midterregering concept.

LA categorically refuses any government with Socialdemokratiet and would be the most combative opposition party if S leads the next government. Their 2024 kursskifte dropped the demand for full topskat abolition in favor of halving it; they propose 55 billion kr in total tax cuts plus nuclear power investment.

**Campaign event:** Vanopslagh admitted to cocaine use during his time as party leader. LA fell in some polls afterwards. A candidate was expelled for publicly criticizing him. The timing was problematic for the campaign's closing days, though LA remains the largest blue party in the weighted polling average.

### 5.9 Danmarksdemokraterne (Stoejberg) -- 13 mandater

DD's single ultimativt krav is a blaa statsminister. Stoejberg is open to pointing to TLP but conditioned on V ruling out making Frederiksen PM -- which TLP has not done. DD is hostile to any midterregering, comparing Venstre's SVM participation to "moving in with the neighbor's wife."

DD has had the worst campaign trajectory, losing ground steadily to DF. 19% of 2022 DD voters now intend to vote DF. Multiple analysts identify DD's decline as the most underappreciated shift of the campaign. DD's key policy signals: 12 billion DKK landdistrikts program funded by ulandsbistand cuts, genindfloerelse of Store Bededag, stram udlaendingepolitik. Personal and political enmity between Stoejberg and Frederiksen (Rigsret conviction) makes S-DD cooperation a hard exclusion.

### 5.10 Dansk Folkeparti (Messerschmidt) -- 14 mandater

DF is polling at a remarkable approximately 9.5% -- a dramatic comeback from its post-2022 existential crisis. DF now demands government seats -- a historic departure from the 2001-2011 stoetteparti model.

**The ultimatum.** Messerschmidt's ultimativt krav is net emigration of Muslims from Denmark written into the regeringsgrundlag. He has explicitly threatened to topple any government that fails to deliver. However, an escape hatch is already visible: DF's Anders Vistisen clarified that the formulation need not be exact, so long as the government platform includes initiatives that produce that consequence. This distinction between demanding the goal versus demanding specific wording creates room for face-saving compromise.

**The escape hatch.** DF EU-parliamentarian Anders Vistisen clarified that the formulation in the regeringsgrundlag need not literally state "net emigration of Muslims" -- it just needs to include initiatives that produce that consequence. This distinction between demanding the goal versus demanding specific wording creates substantial room for face-saving compromise. The historical pattern reinforces skepticism about the toppling threat: DF served as stoetteparti for VK governments for ten consecutive years (2001-2011) without ever toppling or seriously threatening to topple any of them. A Verian poll showed 53% of Danes support the net-emigration demand, giving it political potency but raising the cost of obvious climbdown.

**Hard exclusions:** Categorical rejection of both Frederiksen and Loekke as PM. Rejects the blaa midterregering because it would include M/Loekke. The DF-M mutual veto is the central structural obstacle in blue bloc: any blue government needs DF's support, but M vetoes DF, and DF vetoes M.

### 5.11 Borgernes Parti (Lars Boje Mathiesen) -- 5 mandater

BP is not the renamed Nye Borgerlige. It was founded in 2024 by Lars Boje Mathiesen after his expulsion from Nye Borgerlige. Nye Borgerlige is not on the ballot in 2026.

BP polls at 2.1-3.3% -- right on the threshold. Lars Boje claims the PM candidacy (no other party supports this) and demands remigration as a condition for supporting any government. TLP has publicly excluded BP from government. Positioned with the hardline immigration right, not the blue bloc mainstream.

### 5.12 Alternativet (Rosenkilde) -- 4 mandater

Red-bloc aligned support party. Threshold fragility is the key dynamic (see section 3).

## 6. How negative parliamentarism shapes the 2026 arithmetic

Under Grundloven section 15, a new government takes office through royal appointment after the kongerunde -- the Folketing never votes to approve it. This means a government needs only to not have 90 votes actively cast against it. Abstentions work in the government's favor. The practical implications for 2026 are profound:

- A **red minority government** (S+SF, approximately 61 seats) could govern if EL, RV, ALT, and North Atlantic mandates do not join blue parties in a mistillidsvotum -- even if some refuse formal support.
- An **S+M midterregering** (approximately 47 seats) could govern if no 90-mandate opposition coalition forms. EL could be furious but simply go into opposition without voting no confidence.
- A **blaa midterregering** (V+LA+KF+M, approximately 57 seats) could theoretically govern if S abstains or goes into standard opposition rather than assembling 90 votes against it. But this requires S to choose toleration over active opposition -- an outcome no analyst considers likely.
- Loekke's 2015 precedent is instructive: he governed with just 34 Venstre mandates because DF, LA, and KF passively tolerated rather than actively opposing.

The kongerunde procedure -- where party leaders visit the monarch one by one to recommend a forhandlingsleder -- is where the PM-pointing question becomes binding. Each party leader's note to the King determines the political arithmetic. The first kongerunde under King Frederik X promises to be among the most complex in modern Danish history.

## 7. Historical precedents for 2026 support arrangements

Three precedents from recent Danish history illuminate the mechanics likely to shape the 2026 formation:

### EL-Thorning (2011-2015): no written agreement, constant brinkmanship

EL supported the S-RV-SF government as external stoetteparti with no formal written support agreement. EL negotiated issue-by-issue, primarily through annual finanslov negotiations. The relationship was volatile: in June 2012, after the government bypassed EL to negotiate a tax reform with V and K, EL declared it was "no longer a support party" -- then returned for the 2013 budget. In May 2013, EL's Pernille Skipper was ready to move a mistillidsvotum over the dagpenge crisis; the government averted it with a temporary benefit. In January 2014, the DONG/Goldman Sachs share sale triggered SF's departure from government and EL fury. The experience led EL to adopt new parliamentary principles and to insist on a formal forstaelsespapir in 2019.

### DF-Fogh/Loekke (2001-2011): informal trust, no written agreement

DF's decade-long support operated with strikingly informal mechanics. Pia Kjaersgaard described being shown "a list with some oneliners" -- headlines for the regeringsgrundlag -- and simply accepting. There was no written stoetteaftale. DF's influence flowed through annual finanslov negotiations (Kristian Thulesen Dahl arriving at the Finance Ministry "with yellow post-it notes") and ad hoc immigration legislation. The arrangement was built on personal trust between Kjaersgaard and Fogh. DF now considers this stoetteparti model a mistake -- driving their 2026 demand for government seats.

### The 2019 forstaelsespapir as the modern template

The 18-page "Retfaerdig retning for Danmark" signed by S, RV, SF, and EL on June 25, 2019 was the first of its kind: a political commitment document binding the S government to specific targets (70% CO2 reduction by 2030, daycare staffing norms) in exchange for support party mandates. This is the model EL demands in 2026, but with additional "negative demands" -- things the government must commit to NOT doing (such as breaking international conventions).

## 9. The formueskat as negotiation variable

The formueskat is the single most important policy variable for determining government type (red vs. midter).

**Initial position:** Frederiksen launched the formueskat targeting the wealthiest <1%, generating approximately 6 billion DKK.

**The retreat signal (March 19).** Frederiksen stated she is willing to replace the formueskat: "It is a means to achieve various things... I don't have [a goal] regarding a wealth tax." S has explicitly stated the formueskat is not an ultimative demand. This pre-concession removes the single biggest policy obstacle to S+M cooperation.

**Implication:** Formueskat enacted signals a red government; formueskat dropped signals a midterregering. SF and EL still want it, making it a marker of government type rather than a binding constraint. Alternative progressive revenue measures (aktieskat, kapitalindkomstbeskatning adjustments) are available as replacements.

### Store Bededag reinstatement

Frederiksen said she expects Store Bededag reinstatement to "come into government negotiations" and that "we can easily reinstate it." All opposition parties (red and blue) demand reinstatement. All three SVM parties deny responsibility for the original abolition. This is a broad consensus item that can serve as a coalition-building signal in any direction. SF has stated it should be the first act of a new government.

## 10. Dominant campaign issues and cross-cutting cleavages

The meta-issue of the campaign is coalition formation itself -- who can work with whom after the SVM experiment. But several policy issues shape coalition compatibility:

| Issue | Red-aligned | Blue-aligned | Cross-cutting? |
|-------|-------------|--------------|----------------|
| Formueskat (wealth tax) | S, SF, EL | All blue oppose; M opposes; RV opposes but non-blocking | YES -- dividing line between red and midterregering |

An S+SF+KF+RV+M combination is structurally possible if SF softens its exclusion of KF. The Frederiksen formueskat retreat removes the single biggest policy obstacle to S+M cooperation.

## 11. The kongerunde everyone expects but nobody can script

Analysts universally expect Frederiksen to emerge as kongelig undersoeger -- the person tasked with exploring government options. This will mark the first kongerunde under Frederik X. Frederiksen, as sitting PM, holds the strongest starting position: she controls the process and is under no legal obligation to even hold a kongerunde. No major analyst envisions a scenario where TLP gets first shot.

The decisive variables for the kongerunde, per the analyst consensus: (1) whether red bloc reaches 90 without M; (2) whether ALT clears the spaerregraense; (3) the allocation of North Atlantic mandates; (4) which direction Loekke ultimately swings; and (5) whether SF's demand for government participation proves negotiable.

Analysis drawing on publicly available expert commentary emphasizes that EL's conditional non-support creates a specific dynamic: if EL points to an alternative forhandlingsleder (e.g., Pia Olsen Dyhr, or even Loekke), this does not prevent Frederiksen from negotiating in parallel. EL's "pege-paa" leverage evaporates once a forhandlingsleder is appointed, because the appointed person can negotiate with whomever they choose. The real leverage lies in EL's ability to withhold votes for or against a final government -- not in the kongerunde recommendation itself.

On formation timeline, analysts predict "unheard-of long and unheard-of difficult negotiations." The SVM government's 44-day formation in 2022 was already the longest in Danish history. Several analysts expect this record could be tested again.

## 12. Expert assessment synthesis -- the key scenarios

Expert commentary converges on a handful of realistic formations:

### Scenario arithmetic

| Configuration | Danish mandater (approx.) | Reaches 90? | Loekke-compatible? |
|--------------|--------------------------|-------------|-------------------|
| S+SF+EL+RV+ALT (pure red) | 86 | Only with 4 favorable NA seats | N/A -- M not needed |
| S+SF (+ EL/ALT support) + M | 70 + support | YES if EL tolerates | NO -- M won't enable far-left dependency |
| S+SF+M+RV | ~79-80 | Needs ~10 more from EL tolerance + NA | YES -- M's sweet spot |
| S+M (renewed midterregering) | ~47 | Needs broad tolerance | YES but SF revolts |
| V+LA+KF+M (blaa midterregering) | ~57 | NO | Partially -- needs much more |
| V+LA+KF+DD+DF (pure blue) | ~80 | Only with NA | M vetoes DF |
| SVM continuation | ~64 | NO | Dead on arrival |

### What experts actually discuss

**S+SF (+ external support from EL, RV, ALT, possibly NA):** The "pure red" option. Frederiksen governs with SF as coalition partner, relying on case-by-case support. Only viable if red bloc reaches approximately 88-90 mandater including North Atlantic seats. ValgiDanmark gives this a 24% probability.

**S+SF+M+RV (broad center-left minority government):** What multiple analysts call the most likely outcome if no bloc reaches 90. But it would still require EL and possibly ALT support on the finanslov, quickly becoming unwieldy. The SF-M policy gap makes this extremely difficult to negotiate.

**S+M (renewed midterregering without SF):** Frederiksen's fallback if she prioritizes Loekke's centrist mandates over SF's demands. SF would revolt, and Olsen Dyhr has explicitly warned she will not participate.

**Blaa midterregering (V+LA+KF+M without S):** TLP's late-campaign gambit. Vanopslagh instantly endorsed it. But analysts uniformly flag the DF blocker: Messerschmidt has an absolute veto on anything involving Loekke, and without DF's approximately 14 mandates, the numbers do not add up.

### Convergent conclusions

**Frederiksen is the overwhelming favorite.** Polymarket prices her at approximately 80% to remain PM. The YouGov LSS model finds red + M achieves a majority in >99% of simulations. Holstein calls her the "absolut favorit." No analyst envisions a scenario where TLP gets first shot at the forhandlingsleder role under current polling.

**The SF-M incompatibility is the formation's hardest problem.** Olsen Dyhr and Loekke disagree on 11 of 24 questions in TV 2's candidate test. If Frederiksen pursues a broad midterregering including M, she risks losing SF. But SF's government-or-nothing stance means she cannot keep SF as a support party either.

**A blue government faces near-insurmountable structural obstacles.** The DF-M mutual veto eliminates most viable blue configurations.

**Formation will be long and difficult.** Multiple analysts expect the 2022 record of 44 days to be tested. Analysis drawing on publicly available expert commentary emphasizes that Loekke excels at prolonging negotiations and exploiting ambiguity -- the formation process itself becomes his leverage.

### Key uncertainties ranked by expert frequency

1. **Loekke's direction** -- the single most cited uncertainty. Every commentator uses variants of "tungen paa vaegtskalen" or "kongemager."
2. **Whether ALT clears the 2% threshold** -- genuinely unknown; polls range from 1.6% to 3.1%.
3. **North Atlantic mandate distribution** -- both Greenland incumbents not re-running, making outcomes unprecedented.
4. **Late-deciding voters** -- 12.8% undecided in Voxmeter; high proportion of volatile electorate.
5. **DF's actual behavior vs. campaign rhetoric** -- will Messerschmidt maintain his categorical refusal of Loekke when faced with the alternative of a Frederiksen government?

### Where experts disagree

1. **Frederiksen's certainty as PM:** Holstein says absolut favorit; Mogensen says the question is not who but what kind of PM; Thomas Larsen gives blue bloc more hope. The disagreement is about whether the election is essentially decided or genuinely competitive.

2. **Whether blaa midterregering is viable:** TLP and Vanopslagh promote it; Wagner's analysis of Messerschmidt's book concludes DF is structurally incompatible; Morten Nielsen notes the math barely works even theoretically.

3. **Whether broad government can work again:** Stubager's 1978 analogy suggests distrust kills cross-bloc governments; Rostrup notes SVM proved it "kan vaere effektiv"; Kurrild-Klitgaard's 2022 analysis predicted junior partners would be destroyed (confirmed). Syberg warns broad centre governments produce polarization.

4. **Polling reliability:** Serup is most skeptical; Hjorth flags systematic misreporting bias. Most others treat the approximately 85/78/12 split as roughly reliable while noting statistical margins.

### Risk of instability

Multiple commentators invoke the 1968-1984 cycle (seven elections in ten years). Analysis drawing on publicly available expert commentary warns that a failed blue government would set borgerlige parties back severely. The blue bloc's breadth -- from M to BP -- makes internal agreement extraordinarily difficult. Any government is likely to be born of broken promises; the question is whose promises break first.

### Factors that models miss

Experts identify several dynamics invisible to pure mandate-arithmetic or policy-preference models: (1) personal relationships and trust dynamics (Messerschmidt's hatred of Loekke appears personal, not merely ideological); (2) procedural advantage of the sitting PM (Frederiksen controls sequencing and can skip the kongerunde entirely); (3) timing and sequencing of declarations creating path dependencies; (4) media framing and campaign momentum (feedback loops between polling and voter behavior); (5) the geopolitical shock as a formation variable (Trump/Greenland simultaneously rescued M's poll numbers and empowered North Atlantic bargaining); (6) the distinction between confidence viability and legislative viability (a government can survive but be unable to pass a budget).
